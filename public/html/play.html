Welcome
  • Rules

    This game is known as Atari, or Capture Go. Black starts the game. Each player puts a stone on a square in their turn.

    Stones of the same color are connected in rows and columns. A stone is live as long as it has a connection with an empty square, or when it is the member of the group where at least one other member connects with an empty square. Stone(s) without connection to an empty square are dead, and are captured. The first player who makes a capture, wins.

    If your move would take away the last free square from stones of your own color, it is only allowed if you capture the opposite color as well. If there is no capture until only one empty square is left, the game is drawn.

  • How to play

    To start playing, click on a player's name. When the name is grey, they are waiting for a challenge. If the name is blue, they are currently playing.

    The role of the first and second player is decided randomly. When the game starts, the order of the players's names indicates who plays first.

    You can place a stone to a square by clicking on it.

  • About this game variant
    The board for the original game is different, because the stones are placed on the points, not in the squares. This version is for those who find the chessboard-like 8x8 grid to be a more familiar environment.
