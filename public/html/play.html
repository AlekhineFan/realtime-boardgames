  • Rules

    This game is known as Atari Go (also known as “first-capture Go”). Players move alternately startng with black placing a stone in the middle of any square. The aim of the game is to capture one or more of your opponent’s stones. The first player who makes a capturing move wins the game.

    Capturing single stones

    To capture a stone you need to completely surround it with your stones provided that there is no empty square(s) next to it. Such a stone is said to be dead.

    Capturing connected stones

    A stone which is horizontally or laterally adjacent to another stone of the same colour is said to be connected. To capture connected stones they must all be surrounded by enemy stones and none of them can have an empty square next to them. They are all captured at the same &me.

    No self-capturing

    You are not allowed to make a move which would take away the last free square from your own stones. However if your move captures the opponent’s stone(s) at the same &me then it is allowed and you win the game.

    Draw

    The game is drawn if it reaches the point that there is only one empty square left.

  • How to play

    To start playing, click on a player's name. When the name is grey, they are waiting for a challenge. If the name is blue, they are currently playing. The colours are decided randomly. When the game starts, the order of the names indicates who plays first.

    You place a stone on a square by clicking on it.

  • About this game variant
    Note that in this simplified version of Go. We place the stones on the squares rather than on the intersections. This way of placing pieces is more familiar to the majority of children who use 8x8 boards.
